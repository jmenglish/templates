Problem


Users need a Cloud Storage and Organization tool with features that are user-friendly and provide a significant amount of space to store, share, and collaborate on ideas, documents, and memories.


Solution


The goal for Prism Space is to help users store, organize, and navigate through the ever-evolving space of information we accumulate in our day-to-day personal and professional lives. The product aims to assist you in becoming successful in your endeavors.

 Discovery

User Research


The competitors studied for this project were Google Drive, EverNote, and Dropbox. The research was performed with a user survey sent out to contacts, friends, social media, and subreddit. The focus of the survey was to get an understanding of users current behaviors, preferences, missing features that would be an asset, and demographics of our target audience.

Wireframes


Iterations

The wireframes went through many iterations to determine the content to include and in what format that would best serve the user. The iterations underwent preference tests to narrow down the most functional options.


Homepage

With the home page, two versions were created and tested against each other. In the first version, all of the content was included in a one-page layout to reduce user scrolling, provide company info, product features within view, and entice the user to signup with plan options up front. In the second version, the focus was solely on the product's features and testimonials to entice users to signup. The about and plan/pricing information was moved to a separate page. The preference test showed that a majority of participants preferred a one-page layout regardless of extra scrolling. They wanted to see all content in one location which would minimize clicking, highlight the features, and introduce pricing. With this feedback, the homepage was updated to focus on product features, customer testimonials, and plan details.

Dashboard

There were many versions of the dashboard to narrow down how to display the features appropriately. User testing was utilized to find the optimal layout for users to easily access items for sharing or collaborating. A majority of participants stated that all information in view on the dashboard would simplify navigation making it more accessible by reducing unnecessary steps. Also, it would be easier to organize and find all content within view. These limitations were removed changing the folder structure to give users control of organizing content, naming their content, and by placing all features (upload, create, share, and collaborate) in the immediate view. The design was modified to group actions together in an ellipsis drop-down menu eliminating separate pages.

Dashboard

On the dashboard, participants pointed to steps that did not flow naturally and provided improvements to simplify the process. For example, they had trouble organizing an item because the action icon in the ellipsis menu was not clear to them. Hover text was included to clarify each icon. Also, icons were selected to easily identify each file type (word, excel, PDF,etc.).
Further refinement to the dashboard included removing the files page and combining all content onto the main page to increase productivity. This simplified the dashboard to include all items in one convenient location and allow the user more control in organizing their content.

 User Testing

Summary


There were two types of testing performed for this case study: recorded in person or remote and UsabilityHub preference tests.User testing for this product was performed for desktop only of both the home page and dashboard of this product. In hindsight,testing should have included mobile because the participants on the survey frequently used mobile apps such as Google Drive.


Remote/In-person Testing

For the in-person/remote user testing, the participants completed three tasks: sign up for an account, add a piece of content, and organize a form of content to the dashboard. They were encouraged to speak their thoughts out loud on the experience of navigating through each page. With this approach to testing, I could observe the exact areas of the test participants struggled with and ask prompting questions to collect data.


Perference Testing

For the Preference Test, participants voted on UsabilityHub for their preferred design. User insight was gathered on the content layout, features, or design choices for both the homepage and dashboard. The results were very helpful in guiding the evolution of wireframes and in finalizing design choices.


Findings

While working on the mockups for the homepage, I started questioning the layout of information in the wireframes. After reviewing other website templates to generate ideas, a second design option was created. The second option included more images in a checkered pattern layout with the content. These changes were made to improve the UI design. A preference test was used to gather feedback on which UI experience user's preferred. The results showed that a large majority of participants enjoyed the original layout because it was simple and more natural to read the information. In the end, my doubts about the initial design choice for the homepage was discredited by the participant's approval. Lesson learned!

 Conclusion

Lessons Learned


The assumptions made during this making of this product surrounded on the idea that users would want a tool to not only store data but collaborate on their business and personal ideas. Also, I over looked the amount of users that preferred the mobile app of compeititors over web-based services. The sketches and wireframes were created for web-based initially when they should have been focused on mobile and then branched out.


Next Steps


The next phase of this project would be to return to the mobile designs and focus more user testing on mobile apps. With this data, refine the final designs to include the best features and user experience.
